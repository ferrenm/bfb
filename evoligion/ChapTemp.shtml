Jacket Bio

High-school dropout B. F. Becula never let his seminary training, MIT doctorate, or thalassochemical career interfere with his curiosity. His working years (30~60) taught him that science answered How Come? but not Why?, and that theology answered Why? from an indeterminate base.

He devoted a third of his thinking years (60~90) to Why? before (like many others) accepting projection: gods are very like us. His professorial interpretation has them seeking experimental answers to a difficult problem.

Descended from the bards of the Lord of the Isles, Becula combines Gravesian mythopoiesis and, e.g., the:

  • evolutionary biology of Lynn Margulis,
  • primatology of Allison Jolly,
  • human ecology of Garrett Hardin,
  • psychology of Wilhelm Reich,
  • sociology of Herbert Gintis,
  • geopsychology of James DeMeo,
  • bicamerality of Julian Jaynes,
  • Gaian vision of James Lovelock,
  • Enlightened aspirations of the Founding Fathers,
  • intuition of mathematicians from Plato to Tegmark,
  • and a nod to quantum field theory,

into a novel theory of religion.

Critics may find some of Becula's sources dubious. His reply: ‘They had their problems, but also deep insights. The pont of higher education is to learn to distinguish wheat from chaff.’
