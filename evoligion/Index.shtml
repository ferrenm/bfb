Two-Minute Summary

The hexalogy presents the first theory of religion to accomodate important but overlooked insights of anthropology and psychology. The description below is for those with supple minds but little time. Some of the ideas are likely to be unfamliar and in need of expansion. For this, click on the pointing hand (bottom right), which will take you to a 24-minute chapter-by-chapter summary. Clicking on a book title will take you to that book; on a number, to that chapter.

A. Preludes

35,000 years ago our brains worked differently: (Internal) gods instructed us individually and directly in moments of stress, and we obeyed them willy-nilly.

B. Preoccupation

This is why neither the super-natural content, the sociological benefits, nor prior theories, of religion can explain the strength of its hold on us.

C. Function

Religion increased tribal viability (the fraction of offspring who survive to breed), and in genes and society turned us into time-binding humans.

D. History

Population grew, choices multiplied, rigid rules failed. Over a millenium, our brains adapted. The gods departed silently when no longer needed.

E. Status

The Paleolithic Transition, the 500 BCE Axial Age, the Enlightenment, and the deism of the US Constitution were the high points of religion.

F. Repair

Our ultimate concerns — Tillich's core of religion — are salvation in the sense of living together, and living with our planet. This way survival lies.
